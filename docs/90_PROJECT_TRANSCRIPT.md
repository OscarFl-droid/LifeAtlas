# Project Transcript – Life Atlas Concept Development

## User
The nutritional-memory mechanism generalises well. Replace food sequence with repeated behaviour.

## Assistant
The central insight is not habit tracking. Behaviour has inertia. Order, timing, and repetition alter future state and therefore which intervention is appropriate next. Sequence intelligence, thresholds, temporary states, permanent conditioning, and an evolving Behaviour Atlas were proposed.

## User
The behaviour engine needs to be easy to use and adaptable to diverse users and tasks.

## Assistant
The product was divided into a simple user layer, configurable domain layer, and general adaptive engine. A universal event model, visual rule builder, templates, progressive disclosure, multiple input methods, expert rules, personal rules, and explainable recommendations were defined.

## User
It must track and retain years of data, work offline, be visually appealing, and base coaching on high-quality research and expert recommendations.

## Assistant
These became architectural requirements. The system was reframed as local-first and longitudinal, using an immutable event archive, derived-state cache, evidence registry, recommendation history, and correction history. Evidence was divided into safety rules, population guidance, personal pattern findings, and experimental suggestions. AI was constrained to approved evidence and rules. Audience-specific experience packs were proposed.

## User
Laboratory contamination response is too narrow. Professional development should be tailored to the user's profession and aspirations.

## Assistant
The broad second demonstration became Professional Growth and Career Readiness. The engine should adapt to profession, current role, desired role, aspirations, and demonstrated behaviour. LabAtlas remains a specialist vertical.

## User
Living Atlas is more for health, psychological, social skill, happiness, risk mitigation, professional behaviour, and pattern tracking.

## Assistant
The concept was clarified as a longitudinal human-development platform. Its domains became health, psychological functioning, social capability, happiness/flourishing, risk mitigation, professional development, and purpose/growth. Nodes should have evidence, confidence, trajectory, stability, and dependencies. Cross-domain feedback loops are central.

## Current canonical direction
Life Atlas is offline-first, longitudinal, evidence-governed, explainable, visually engaging, adaptable by profession and demographic, and non-diagnostic.
